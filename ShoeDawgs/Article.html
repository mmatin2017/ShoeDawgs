The Nike Air Jordan 1

Air Jordan, His Airness, The GOAT, MJ, Mike, Michael Jordan. We all know who he is, and his tremendous accomplishments on the court, but his impact off the court and in the world of business is what truly separates him and from the pack. Michael Jordan’s impact on “street culture” and the shoe industry is unparalleled and the legacy that he’s created will live on well into the future.


The Air Jordan 1 is arguably the most iconic shoe in sneaker history. 35 years after the initial release, the silhouette is still one of the most sought after in the sneakerhead community. No matter if the shoe is in limited quantity or for general release, the hype for these shoes are always there, making them harder to obtain than the average sneaker. In the resale market, depending on the colorway the shoe will often times sell for more than what it was retailed for, sometimes even selling for well over a thousand dollars. The countless colorways give the average consumer a variety to choose from in their liking and the many different collaborations adds to the hype for the more avid sneakerhead. This has led to the Air Jordan 1 being appreciated by millions throughout the years.


Nike was not Michael Jordan’s first choice for a shoe endorsement. Instead, he was more interested in signing a deal with Adidas or Converse. However, Adidas were not attentive at the time to sign a young Michael Jordan due to the passing away the founder of the company, Adi Dassler. Furthermore, Converse had overlooked Michael as they had shoe deals with Larry Bird and Magic Johnson. The reason he was not keen on signing with Nike as he felt that he had no court feel in the shoes that Nike had at the time. However, the company aggressively pursued the youngster, and offered a new shoe tailored just for him. Having a tailored shoe along with the large contract that Nike was providing sealed the deal, thus leading to the creation of the most iconic shoe in sneaker culture.
